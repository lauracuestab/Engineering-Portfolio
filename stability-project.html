Aircraft Stability Analysis & Flight Simulation

Designed, analyzed, and simulated a fixed-wing aircraft using classical aircraft stability theory, MATLAB, USAF Digital DATCOM, Simulink, and FlightGear. The project combined analytical calculations, computational validation, and dynamic flight simulation to evaluate the aircraft's stability and overall flight performance.

Project Overview

This semester-long project involved the complete design, analysis, and simulation of a fixed-wing aircraft to evaluate its longitudinal, lateral, and directional stability. Beginning with a proposed aircraft geometry, classical aircraft stability and control theory was used to analytically determine the aerodynamic coefficients and stability derivatives governing the aircraft's behavior. Every calculation was performed by hand following the methods presented throughout the course and independently verified in Microsoft Excel to ensure numerical accuracy and consistency.

The provided MATLAB scripts were modified to generate the aircraft geometry and aerodynamic properties required by USAF Digital DATCOM. Using the same aircraft configuration, Digital DATCOM predicted aerodynamic coefficients for multiple flight conditions, providing a higher-fidelity aerodynamic model that could be directly compared against the analytical calculations. The theoretical and computational results were organized and analyzed in Excel to evaluate the accuracy of the analytical methods and validate the aircraft design.

After confirming that the aircraft satisfied the required longitudinal, lateral, and directional stability criteria, the aerodynamic coefficients generated by Digital DATCOM were incorporated into a provided MATLAB/Simulink flight dynamics model through aerodynamic lookup tables. The simulation environment was modified to represent the designed aircraft and connected to FlightGear, where the completed model successfully demonstrated stable flight behavior under various control inputs.

Project Development

The project was completed in three sequential deliverables, each building upon the previous phase. This development process closely followed a typical aerospace engineering workflow, progressing from analytical aircraft design to computational validation and finally full flight simulation. Each deliverable documents the engineering methodology, calculations, and validation completed during that stage of the project.

Deliverable I
Aircraft Design & Longitudinal Stability

The first phase established the aircraft geometry and focused on longitudinal stability analysis. Lift, drag, downwash, pitching moment, and other aerodynamic coefficients were determined through analytical hand calculations based on aircraft stability theory. Every calculation was independently verified in Microsoft Excel before being compared against USAF Digital DATCOM predictions for the selected flight condition.

Includes aircraft geometry development, longitudinal stability calculations, Excel verification, and DATCOM validation.

Deliverable II
Lateral & Directional Stability Analysis

The second phase expanded the analysis to include lateral and directional stability. Stability derivatives associated with the fuselage, wing, vertical tail, and control surfaces were calculated to evaluate roll and yaw behavior. The aircraft's neutral point was determined to verify static stability, while Digital DATCOM results were used to validate the analytical predictions.

Includes lateral and directional stability calculations, derivative validation, neutral point analysis, and comparison with Digital DATCOM.

Deliverable III
Flight Simulation & Validation

The final phase integrated the validated Digital DATCOM aerodynamic coefficients into a provided MATLAB/Simulink flight dynamics model using aerodynamic lookup tables. The simulation environment was modified to represent the designed aircraft and connected to FlightGear, where the aircraft successfully demonstrated stable flight during simulated flight testing.

Includes Simulink implementation, aerodynamic lookup tables, flight simulation, FlightGear visualization, and final validation.

Engineering Process

The project followed a structured engineering workflow, progressing from the initial aircraft design through analytical calculations, computational validation, and finally full flight simulation. Each phase built upon the previous one to develop, verify, and validate the aircraft model.

Aircraft Geometry

The project began by defining the aircraft geometry, including the wing, fuselage, horizontal tail, and vertical tail dimensions. These geometric properties served as the foundation for every aerodynamic calculation, stability analysis, and simulation performed throughout the project.

Analytical Hand Calculations

Longitudinal, lateral, and directional stability derivatives were calculated using classical aircraft stability and control theory presented throughout the course. These calculations established the theoretical aerodynamic behavior of the aircraft before any computational tools were introduced.

The complete analytical work was organized into three project deliverables. Each report documents the engineering methodology, calculations, assumptions, and validation completed during that phase.

Excel Verification

Every analytical calculation was independently reproduced in Microsoft Excel to verify numerical accuracy, automate repetitive calculations, and compare analytical predictions with USAF Digital DATCOM results. The workbook contains separate worksheets for geometry calculations, aerodynamic coefficients, stability derivatives, and result comparisons.

USAF Digital DATCOM

The aircraft geometry generated in MATLAB was analyzed using USAF Digital DATCOM to predict aerodynamic coefficients over multiple flight conditions. These higher-fidelity predictions were compared with the analytical calculations to validate the aircraft design.

A MATLAB script was then used to process the Digital DATCOM output files and automatically generate aerodynamic performance plots for comparison and analysis.

Validation & Comparison

Theoretical aerodynamic coefficients obtained from the analytical calculations were compared with the Digital DATCOM predictions using Microsoft Excel. This comparison verified the analytical methods, quantified differences between the theoretical and computational models, and confirmed that the aircraft satisfied the required stability criteria.

Simulink Flight Model

Once the aircraft demonstrated acceptable longitudinal, lateral, and directional stability characteristics, the validated Digital DATCOM aerodynamic coefficients were incorporated into a provided MATLAB/Simulink flight dynamics model using aerodynamic lookup tables. The simulation environment was modified to accurately represent the designed aircraft and prepared for dynamic flight simulation.

Flight Validation

The completed aircraft model was connected to FlightGear for visualization and dynamic flight testing, as demonstrated in the video at the top of this page. Multiple control inputs and flight conditions were evaluated to verify stable longitudinal, lateral, and directional behavior. The successful simulation confirmed the implementation of the aircraft model and validated the analytical and computational work completed throughout the project.
