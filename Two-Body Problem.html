Two-Body Orbit Analysis

Developed an original MATLAB program to perform classical orbit analysis of an Earth-orbiting satellite. The project computed orbital elements, ground-station observations, and propagated the orbit using numerical integration with ODE45 to validate satellite motion and conservation of orbital energy.

Project Overview

This project focused on developing an original MATLAB program to perform a complete two-body orbital analysis from a given position and velocity vector. The program calculated the six classical orbital elements, determined satellite visibility from a ground station through range, azimuth, and elevation calculations, and propagated the orbit using MATLAB's ODE45 numerical solver. Engineering Highlights

Orbit Propagation

Using the calculated orbital elements, a numerical two-body propagator was developed in MATLAB with the ODE45 solver to simulate the satellite trajectory over two complete orbital periods. The resulting three-dimensional orbit confirmed the expected elliptical trajectory and correctly identified the satellite position at apoapsis.

Ground Station Analysis

Coordinate transformations between the Earth-Centered Inertial (ECI) and South-East-Zenith (SEZ) reference frames were implemented to determine the satellite's range, azimuth, and elevation relative to the Boston Air Force Station. These calculations provided the ground-station observations needed to evaluate satellite visibility.

Model Validation

The propagated orbit was validated by monitoring the specific orbital energy and angular momentum throughout the simulation. Both quantities remained nearly constant during propagation, confirming the accuracy of the numerical integration and demonstrating the expected behavior of an ideal two-body system.
